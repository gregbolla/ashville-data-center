A developer has proposed building a large-scale data center in Ashville, Ohio. To power it, the project also requires an 800 megawatt natural gas generating facility — a project of sufficient scale that it must clear the Ohio Power Siting Board before construction can begin.

## What Has Been Approved

The Ashville Town Council has approved the data center. Local land use and zoning for the facility have been cleared at the municipal level.

## What Is Still Pending

The 800 MW gas power plant requires a **Certificate of Environmental Compatibility and Public Need** from the Ohio Power Siting Board (OPSB). This is a separate regulatory process from local approval.

The OPSB reviews:
- Environmental impacts
- Need for the power
- Effect on the reliability of the electric grid
- Impact on surrounding communities

The board holds mandatory public hearings and accepts public comment during the review process.

## Why It Matters

800 MW is a significant amount of generation capacity — comparable to a mid-sized coal plant. Siting a new gas plant of this scale has implications for Ohio's energy mix, local air quality, and long-term infrastructure commitments at a time when the state and utilities are navigating energy transition questions.

This site will track filings, hearing dates, and developments as the OPSB process moves forward.
